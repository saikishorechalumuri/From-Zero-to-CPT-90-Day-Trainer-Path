# MODULE 1 Know If Training Is For You Before CPT  
If you are new to the training industry and want to start first check if this job fits you  
Training is not just workouts it is patience consistency communication and service  

Section 1 What this job requires daily  
1 Patience and calm when clients repeat mistakes  
2 Communication that is simple clear and respectful  
3 Energy and presence even on slow days  
4 Discipline to show up prepared every shift  
5 Caring about people not just programs  
6 Willingness to learn and accept feedback  
7 Ability to sell ethically and ask for commitment  
8 Ability to handle rejection and stay confident  
9 Ability to track progress and follow systems  
10 Ability to build trust over time not overnight  

Section 2 Quick self test  
If you can do these you will do well  
1 You enjoy helping people improve  
2 You can explain things simply  
3 You can stay calm with difficult people  
4 You are okay with slow progress  
5 You are ready to follow a system not freestyle  
6 You are comfortable talking about money professionally  
7 You can take feedback without ego  
8 You can work evenings weekends and peak hours  
9 You can stay consistent for months  
10 You can handle no show and still keep standards

# MODULE 2 Career Path To Get Into The Industry  
There are multiple entry doors  
Choose one based on your current skills  

Path A Front Desk MSR to Trainer  
Step 1 Get hired as front desk learn customer service retention and systems  
Step 2 Shadow trainers and help with tours and onboarding  
Step 3 Start assisting sessions and complete CPT in probation  

Path B Fitness Floor Coach to Trainer  
Step 1 Get hired on the floor learn safety form basics and member engagement  
Step 2 Start booking consultations and shadow assessments  
Step 3 Move into junior trainer role and complete CPT  

Path C Co Op Junior Trainer Path  
Step 1 Apply for co op junior trainer supervised role  
Step 2 Deliver sessions under review build client files and tracking  
Step 3 Complete CPT within 90 days  

Path D Portfolio First Path  
Step 1 Build proof pack before applying  
Step 2 Apply with program templates assessment checklist and case studies  
Step 3 Start under mentorship and finish CPT in probation

# MODULE 4 Interview Modules How To Get Hired  
Managers hire safety coachability systems and professionalism  

Section 1 The five step answer system  
Step 1 Acknowledge and show empathy  
Step 2 Ask two clarifying questions  
Step 3 Give three options you can do now  
Step 4 Confirm next step and timeline  
Step 5 Document and close the loop  

Section 2 What to say clearly  
1 I can follow systems and document cleanly  
2 I am coachable and I want feedback weekly  
3 I will complete CPT within 90 days  
4 I can support sales pipeline ethically  
5 I will protect member experience and club standards  

Section 3 Top scenarios you must practice  
1 Client wants fat loss but refuses habit changes  
2 Client has knee pain during squat  
3 Client cancels and no shows  
4 Client wants results in two weeks  
5 Client is nervous and lacks confidence  
6 Client argues with the program  
7 Plateau after four weeks  
8 Client asks medical advice outside scope  
9 Client unhappy with scheduling  
10 You made a mistake how do you fix it

# MODULE 5 After Getting The Job What You Do In Probation  
Probation is where you earn trust  
Your goal is to become reliable and Level 1 ready  

Section 1 Day 1 to Day 30 Foundation  
1 Shadow minimum 10 sessions  
2 Deliver sessions using the club format  
3 Build client files and notes  
4 Learn onboarding and assessment process  
5 Practice cueing regressions progressions  

Section 2 Day 31 to Day 60 Results and retention  
1 Weekly check ins with every client  
2 Track three metrics per client strength habit and progress  
3 Improve session delivery and time control  
4 Use renewal conversations professionally  
5 Follow up all leads and consultations  

Section 3 Day 61 to Day 90 Business readiness  
1 Participate in consultations and kickoffs  
2 Book show close follow up consistently  
3 Maintain clean notes and tracking  
4 Ask for referrals and testimonials ethically  
5 Prepare for CPT exam and schedule it

# MODULE 6 CPT Certification Options North America  
These are common top options in North America  
Choose based on your goal and where you want to work  

1 NASM  
Known for structured content and corrective exercise focus  
Good for trainers who want strong programming and assessment structure  

2 ISSA  
Known for flexibility and online study options  
Good for people balancing work and study  

3 ACE  
Known for broad credibility and practical coaching approach  
Good for general fitness coaching and lifestyle focus  

4 canfitpro Canada  
Commonly recognized in Canadian gyms  
Good if your target is Canadian club employment  

Note There are other bodies but these four are widely known and commonly accepted  
Ask the club you are applying to which certifications they recognize before you pay for an exam

# MODULE 7 Simple 90 Day CPT Plan  
Week 1 CPR AED complete  
Week 2 Anatomy and movement basics  
Week 3 Program design basics  
Week 4 Coaching cues and regressions  
Week 5 Client psychology adherence retention  
Week 6 Consult flow objections and sales basics  
Week 7 Mock exams weak area repair  
Week 8 Mock exams refinement  
Week 9 Final review and exam readiness  
Week 10 CPT exam completion window  

Daily rule  
60 minutes study 5 days a week  
1 mock exam or quiz day weekly  
2 case studies written weekly linked to what you studied
